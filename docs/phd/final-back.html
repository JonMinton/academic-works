Appendix: The Screening Tool

As a result of a request for information I made, which was initially rejected, then only acted upon when I responded by mentioning, explicitly, the Freedom of Information Act, I received the following information about how the Screening Tool works on 13 May 2008:

A score will be produced based on the responses to the screening questions. The higher the score, the more likely it is that the claimant will return to work within a year from the start of their Incapacity Benefit claim. If the score is above the threshold value of 1.11, then the claimant will be screened out and not required to attend any additional WFI’s. However, if the score is below the threshold they will still be required to attend additional WFI’s.

The variables that are important in predicting whether or not a claimant should be screened in or out are each given a parameter value. The score will be obtained by giving the claimants’ response a value; 1 for a positive response or 0 for a negative response. This is then multiplied by the appropriate parameter and totalled to give the final score.

For example, a person who expects to work within 3 months and their last job was ‘clerical and secretarial’, but who did not match any of the other entries in the table, would have a score of 1.9514 (0.7599 + 1.1915). As this is greater than the 1.11 threshold, this claimant would not be required to attend further WFI’s.
