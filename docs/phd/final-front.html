Work and its Other: A Multipleperspective Analysis of Incapacity Benefit Reform

Jonathan William Minton Department of Sociology July 2009

Abstract

This is a thesis of two halves: five „theory‟ chapters and five „empirical‟ chapters, prefaced by an introduction, and then followed by a conclusion. The five „empirical‟ chapters are based around understanding the development and rollout of a programme of Incapacity Benefits reform known as „Pathways to Work‟, which was first introduced in 2002. Pathways to Work has since been implemented in all Jobcentre Plus offices across the UK, and so this half of the thesis could be seen as a case study of a „successfully implemented‟ government policy. The five „theoretical‟ chapters introduce, explain, and interpret a range of theories about social processes that operate at various temporal and spatial scales. These theories range from the very small scale (human neurobiology) to the very large scale (archaeology and biogeography), and conclude with mid-scale theories relating to observed and intuitive everyday interpersonal interactions. The two halves of the thesis are intended to be complementary, with the „theoretical‟ half illuminating the „empirical‟ half and the „empirical‟ half illuminating the „theoretical‟ half. The thesis concludes by offering a number of policy suggestions, and making the complementarity between thesis halves more explicit.
