Pre-PhD research document (2006)

“Simulating Geographies of Homelessness and Worklessness”

Mapping jobs, homes, and historical legacies onto the ages of old age

This extended document was written during the transition from MA to PhD (2005–2006). It represents the early theoretical direction for the PhD — an ambitious attempt to bridge complexity theory, behavioural economics, and critical social theory to understand spatial disadvantage. The PhD later pivoted from this direction towards an analysis of the Pathways to Work programme.

The document is explicitly a work in progress, with visible authorial notes, structural uncertainty, and bracketed working commentary preserved as-is.

Chapters

  1. Preface — Personal context and writing process
  2. Concepts — How home and work map onto pension and place
  3. House and Home — Home as multi-valued asset; chaos and catastrophe theory
  4. Work and Worklessness — Work as community worthiness; relative deprivation
  5. Heuristics and Attitudes — Cognitive psychology and public attitudes to non-working populations
  6. Chaos and Complexity — Chaotic, complex, and complicated phenomena
  7. Statistical Theory as Model — Gary King’s statistical modelling approaches
  8. Literature Review — Extensive reading notes (Bourdieu, Ball, Diamond, Wilkinson)
  9. Bibliography

Key themes

Complexity Theory Behavioural Economics Kahneman & Tversky Homelessness Worklessness Bourdieu Wilkinson
